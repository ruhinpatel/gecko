## Summary for today

Today I used GPT-5.2 to develop a Trame application to visualize **basis-set errors** in the unit-sphere (direction-dependent) representation of the SHG hyperpolarizability $\beta$. The specific view I am using is a direct comparison of two basis sets—**Basis A: aug-cc-pVDZ** and **Basis B: d-aug-cc-pCVDZ**—against a fixed reference (**mra-high**), using the same unit-sphere sampling and globally consistent color limits.

### What the visualization shows

At each direction $\hat{n}$ on the unit sphere, the $\beta$ tensor is mapped to an “effective-vector” field $\mathbf{v}(\hat{n})\in\mathbb{R}^3$. The basis-set error field is
$$
\Delta\mathbf{v}(\hat{n}) = \mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{bas}}(\hat{n}) - \mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ref}}(\hat{n}).
$$

To emphasize **signed, physically interpretable errors**, the basis vectors are colored by the **signed parallel component** of the error relative to the *reference* direction. Define the reference unit direction
$$
\hat{u}(\hat{n}) = \frac{\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ref}}(\hat{n})}{\|\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ref}}(\hat{n})\|+\epsilon},
$$
and the signed parallel error
$$
\Delta v_{\parallel}(\hat{n}) = \Delta\mathbf{v}(\hat{n})\cdot \hat{u}(\hat{n}).
$$

The displayed scalar is the **parallel-relative signed error**
$$
\mathrm{par\_rel\_signed}(\hat{n}) = \frac{\Delta v_{\parallel}(\hat{n})}{\mathrm{ref\_scale}+\epsilon},
$$
where `ref_scale` is a *global* reference magnitude (by default the Lebedev-weighted RMS of $\|\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ref}}\|$ over the sphere). Positive values indicate local **overestimation** along the reference direction; negative values indicate **underestimation**.

On the right side, a fixed 3-panel plot summarizes the basis-set errors across the **D/T/Q sequence**. Together, the sphere fields (spatial structure) and the metric plots (global summaries) provide a compact description of how each basis set differs from the reference.

## The three metrics (what they mean)

All sphere integrals are evaluated with Lebedev quadrature weights (solid-angle integration on the unit sphere).

1) **Relative vector-field $L_2$ error (`rel_L2`)**

This measures the global RMS magnitude of the vector error, normalized by the global RMS magnitude of the reference field:
$$
\mathrm{rel\_}L_2
= \frac{\mathrm{RMS}(|\Delta\mathbf{v}|)}{\mathrm{RMS}(|\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ref}}|)+\epsilon}
= \frac{\sqrt{\frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\Omega} \|\Delta\mathbf{v}(\hat{n})\|^2\, d\Omega}}{\sqrt{\frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\Omega} \|\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ref}}(\hat{n})\|^2\, d\Omega}+\epsilon}.
$$

2) **Signed positive/negative $L_2$ contributions of `par_rel_signed`**

To separate over- vs underestimation, we split `par_rel_signed` into positive and negative regions and compute separate RMS-$L_2$ values:
$$
L2_{+} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\Omega} \max(\mathrm{par\_rel\_signed}(\hat{n}), 0)^2\, d\Omega},
\qquad
L2_{-} = -\sqrt{\frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\Omega} \min(\mathrm{par\_rel\_signed}(\hat{n}), 0)^2\, d\Omega}.
$$
($L2_{-}$ is shown as negative only so it plots below zero.)

3) **Mean angle error (`mean_ang`)**

This is the solid-angle average of the angular deviation between basis and reference effective vectors:
$$
\mathrm{mean\_ang} = \frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\Omega} \angle\big(\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{bas}}(\hat{n}),\,\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ref}}(\hat{n})\big)\, d\Omega.
$$
